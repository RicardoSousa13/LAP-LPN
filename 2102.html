todas as noites, Pedro Magalhães

Todas as noites is part of Pedro Magalhães’s photographic archive, bringing together ritualistic images that represent situations which have been casually repeating and recurring since 2008. Based on a selection of photographs, an exhibition is curated that is understood as a work - akin to Chopin’s view that music is the child of the night, both hypnotically melancholic and intensely fleeting. Todas as noites symbolises the tempo and timbre of the image, akin to the pulse of a body that feeds on the night and the light, and endures.

An archive of analogue photographs, taken with a compact camera, which reflects the importance of the time between the shutter being released and the moment the film is developed, sometimes several years later. The images remain on the rolls, in the night, in a space of latency, waiting to be developed and become clear. The aim here is not to create an archive, but rather to record, much like a pocket diary, situations, circumstances and moments that thrive on the spontaneity and chance of a click - You Press the Button, We Do the Rest (Kodak’s 1888 slogan).

The collection of images on display is intensified by the memory that the photograph itself carries, as if they were a stylistic device, an analepsis. Within the same archive, one can identify similar situations in different contexts, or the same theme depicted years apart. There are many brewery aquariums, solitary cigarettes, hanging octopuses, bones in pipes, and people who are not there, yet inhabit the images.
