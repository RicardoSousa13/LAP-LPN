Louro e Bambu – segundo acto, figuração do exótico

Louro e Bambu – segundo momento, figuração do exótico, is a curatorial project that took place at CAAA, Guimarães* – following exhibition of the project “It was only a moment for you”, by Candice Lin. A complex net of references linked with the notion of verticality and horizontality on post colonial theories, based on documents such as Chris Marker’s film Sans Soleil (1983), or the Poetic of Relations by Edouard Glissant (1997).

Thereby the second act of the curatorial project – Bay leafs and bambu, sets the possibility for different interpretations concerning post colonial theories and artistic manifestations, matured and developed given the present context of our world politics, information management and access – proper within the context of the 21st century. The gaze trough the past is no longer so polluted or circumscribed. One can not be so restrained to a small amount of theory, anthropology and ethnography written on this issue. We tend to have either a superficial or conservative perspective on colonialism. Taking this under consideration, as curators, we have to acknowledge the personal experience of the exhibiting authors, and their views on the concept of the exotic provided from the discoveries of this enchanting New World.

Exotic can have several meanings, from pole dancing clubs, to the amazing vacations in Mallorca (its most dodgy reference). The artefact that lays on the shelf on the fireplace. Even kryptonite is considered exotic. No one really got it… but the truth is that Super Man died because of it, and no one doubts that fact!

One can say the exotic is never at home. The displacement of the object, of concepts, even of rituals and traditions, that are always being re-contextualised within the circumstances and societies, are activated or presented, therefore, assuming new meanings according to physical and political contexts.

The exhibition was not a reflection on the tribal exotic, but a view on ‘singular exotic’, from the perspective of the invited artists and their imaginary constructions of the Exotic. The 21th century Exotic is acknowledge, alongside telluric, sacred and celtic, some of the different exotics figured on the show. In this case, the artworks when activated functioned as an installation, invading the human senses, from the reverberation of a chorus to the smell of a time lapse, kind of in a mantric form.

Taking under consideration the ‘singular exotic’ of our six collaborating artists, Bay leafs and bambu represented a overwhelming figuration of its notion. The aim of its curation was to engage the audience on a throbbing, mind-blowing sensation, that took over all of the human senses. The artists developed their own representation of the Exotic, conveying the exhibition into a trans-sensorial experience, that aimed to subvert the idea of exotic and the subjective lexicon associated to it.
